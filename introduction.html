Introduction

Biomedical research has grown into one of the most rapidly expanding and data-rich domains in modern science, with more than 40 million records indexed in PubMed and millions of new publications added annually. This expansion reflects major advances across fields such as neuroscience, immunology, genetics, and public health, but it also introduces substantial challenges. As the volume of scientific output accelerates, it becomes increasingly difficult to understand how knowledge evolves, which topics gain momentum, and what factors contribute to influential discoveries. Recent large-scale analyses highlight the scale of this growth; for example, a 2025 study of 1.74 million full-text biomedical articles across 2,744 journals found that publication activity has intensified sharply since 2015, particularly in interdisciplinary areas that blend computation with biology (https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.12821). This project explores biomedical research as a complex system using data science techniques to analyze publication trends, collaboration structures, and thematic development across scientific literature. The primary goal is to leverage large-scale academic databases to identify how research topics change over time, how scientific communities form around specific areas, and which characteristics are associated with impactful research. By examining patterns across millions of publications, this study aims to provide a structured view of how biomedical knowledge is produced and how scientific fields evolve. Ultimately, this work seeks to better understand the dynamics of scientific progress itself using computational and statistical tools.

The rapid growth of biomedical literature has also introduced challenges related to information overload and knowledge fragmentation. Researchers often face difficulty keeping up with the sheer volume of new publications appearing each year across different journals and disciplines, increasing the risk that important findings may be overlooked or duplicated. At the same time, emerging areas of research can expand rapidly. For instance, recent analyses show that approximately 3.77% of biomedical articles now contain AI-related content, with especially strong growth in radiology, medical informatics, and computational biology. These thematic surges illustrate how scientific attention can shift quickly, reshaping the structure of the research landscape. Scientific research is also shaped by external forces such as technological advances, global health events, and changes in funding priorities. Major events can rapidly redirect scientific attention; for example, the COVID‑19 pandemic produced one of the most dramatic shifts in biomedical publishing, causing sharp temporary increases in citation metrics for leading medical journals and altering publication patterns across multiple fields. These disruptions demonstrate how flexible and responsive the research ecosystem can be, with new problems prompting immediate surges in scientific output. Understanding how these external influences affect publication trends provides insight into which areas of science are most sensitive to global challenges and how research priorities evolve over time.

Another important aspect of biomedical research is the role of collaboration in shaping scientific discovery. Modern research is rarely conducted by isolated individuals and instead relies on teams of scientists working across institutions, countries, and disciplines. Large-scale analyses show that collaboration intensity has increased steadily in recent years; for example, the median number of authors per biomedical paper rose from 6.15 in 2016 to 6.90 in 2023, accompanied by a parallel rise in the number of contributing institutions. These trends reflect the growing importance of interdisciplinary teamwork in addressing complex scientific questions. Large collaborative projects, such as international disease studies or genome mapping initiatives, often produce highly influential results due to their scale and diversity, while smaller research groups continue to drive innovation in specialized areas. Understanding how collaboration patterns influence research impact provides valuable insight into how scientific knowledge is collectively built and disseminated.

Research Questions

  1. Which biomedical research topics have grown the fastest over the past decade?
  2. What are the most common themes or keywords in high-impact biomedical papers?
  3. How do publication trends differ across major biomedical fields (e.g., neuroscience, oncology, immunology)?
  4. What characteristics (authors, journal type, publication year, collaboration size) are associated with highly cited papers?
  5. Do papers with more authors or institutions tend to receive more citations?
  6. How do collaboration networks between researchers and institutions influence research impact?
  7. Can biomedical papers be grouped into meaningful clusters based on their abstracts and keywords?
  8. Are certain journals consistently associated with higher-impact research outputs?
  9. Can we predict whether a paper will become highly cited based on early publication features?
  10. How has the focus of biomedical research shifted in response to major global events or emerging scientific challenges?
