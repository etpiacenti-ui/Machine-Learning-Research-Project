Introduction

Biomedical research has grown into one of the most rapidly expanding and data-rich domains in modern science, producing millions of publications across areas such as neuroscience, immunology, genetics, and public health. As this body of literature continues to grow, it becomes increasingly difficult to understand how scientific knowledge evolves, which topics are gaining momentum, and what factors contribute to influential discoveries. While individual studies provide insights into specific questions, the broader structure of biomedical research—how ideas emerge, spread, and influence future work—remains less clearly understood. A systematic, data-driven approach can help reveal patterns across large collections of scientific papers that are not easily observable through traditional literature review methods.

This project explores biomedical research as a complex system using data science techniques to analyze publication trends, collaboration structures, and thematic development across scientific literature. By leveraging large-scale academic databases, the goal is to identify how research topics change over time, how scientific communities form around specific areas, and what characteristics are associated with impactful research. Through clustering, classification, and predictive modeling, this study aims to provide a structured view of how biomedical knowledge is produced and how scientific fields evolve. Ultimately, this work seeks to better understand the dynamics of scientific progress itself using computational and statistical tools.

Research Questions

  1. Which biomedical research topics have grown the fastest over the past decade?
  2. What are the most common themes or keywords in high-impact biomedical papers?
  3. How do publication trends differ across major biomedical fields (e.g., neuroscience, oncology, immunology)?
  4. What characteristics (authors, journal type, publication year, collaboration size) are associated with highly cited papers?
  5. Do papers with more authors or institutions tend to receive more citations?
  6. How do collaboration networks between researchers and institutions influence research impact?
  7. Can biomedical papers be grouped into meaningful clusters based on their abstracts and keywords?
  8. Are certain journals consistently associated with higher-impact research outputs?
  9. Can we predict whether a paper will become highly cited based on early publication features?
  10. How has the focus of biomedical research shifted in response to major global events or emerging scientific challenges?
