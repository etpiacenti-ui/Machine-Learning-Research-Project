Introduction

Biomedical research has grown into one of the most rapidly expanding and data-rich domains in modern science, producing millions of publications across areas such as neuroscience, immunology, genetics, and public health. As this body of literature continues to grow, it becomes increasingly difficult to understand how scientific knowledge evolves, which topics are gaining momentum, and what factors contribute to influential discoveries. While individual studies provide insights into specific questions, the broader structure of biomedical research—how ideas emerge, spread, and influence future work—remains less clearly understood. A systematic, data-driven approach can help reveal patterns across large collections of scientific papers that are not easily observable through traditional literature review methods. This project explores biomedical research as a complex system using data science techniques to analyze publication trends, collaboration structures, and thematic development across scientific literature. The primary goal is to leverage large-scale academic databases to identify how research topics change over time, how scientific communities form around specific areas, and which characteristics are associated with impactful research. This study aims to provide a structured view of how biomedical knowledge is produced and how scientific fields evolve. Ultimately, this work seeks to better understand the dynamics of scientific progress itself using computational and statistical tools.

The rapid growth of biomedical literature has also introduced challenges related to information overload and knowledge fragmentation. Researchers often face difficulty keeping up with the sheer volume of new publications appearing each year across different journals and disciplines. This can lead to important findings being overlooked or duplicated across unrelated fields. At the same time, emerging areas of research can be difficult to identify early, before they become widely established. Understanding how scientific attention shifts over time may help address these challenges by revealing patterns in how topics gain visibility and importance. It may also provide insight into how interdisciplinary connections form between fields such as genetics, neuroscience, and public health. As shown in Figure 2, biomedical research spans a highly interconnected landscape of disciplines, highlighting both the diversity of the field and the structural relationships that shape scientific discovery.

Scientific research is also shaped by external forces such as technological advances, global health events, and changes in funding priorities. For example, major events such as disease outbreaks or breakthroughs in sequencing technologies can significantly shift the focus of biomedical research. These shifts often lead to rapid increases in publications within certain fields, followed by longer-term changes in research direction. Understanding how these external influences affect scientific output can provide insight into how flexible and responsive the research ecosystem is. It also helps identify which areas of science are most sensitive to global challenges and emerging needs. By analyzing publication trends over time, it becomes possible to observe how science adapts to new problems and opportunities. This type of analysis can also highlight the role of collaboration networks in accelerating discovery during periods of rapid change. Overall, studying these patterns provides a deeper understanding of how biomedical research evolves in response to both internal scientific development and external world events.

Another important aspect of biomedical research is the role of collaboration in shaping scientific discovery. Modern research is rarely conducted by isolated individuals and instead relies on teams of scientists working across institutions, countries, and disciplines. These collaborations allow for the sharing of expertise, resources, and data, which can accelerate the pace of discovery and improve the quality of scientific findings. At the same time, differences in collaboration size and structure may influence how widely research is recognized or cited within the scientific community. Large collaborative projects, such as international disease studies or genome mapping initiatives, often produce highly influential results due to their scale and scope. However, smaller research groups can also contribute highly innovative or specialized findings that drive new directions in science. Understanding how collaboration patterns affect research impact can provide insight into how scientific knowledge is collectively built. This perspective helps frame biomedical research not just as individual studies, but as a coordinated global effort to advance human health and understanding.

Research Questions

  1. Which biomedical research topics have grown the fastest over the past decade?
  2. What are the most common themes or keywords in high-impact biomedical papers?
  3. How do publication trends differ across major biomedical fields (e.g., neuroscience, oncology, immunology)?
  4. What characteristics (authors, journal type, publication year, collaboration size) are associated with highly cited papers?
  5. Do papers with more authors or institutions tend to receive more citations?
  6. How do collaboration networks between researchers and institutions influence research impact?
  7. Can biomedical papers be grouped into meaningful clusters based on their abstracts and keywords?
  8. Are certain journals consistently associated with higher-impact research outputs?
  9. Can we predict whether a paper will become highly cited based on early publication features?
  10. How has the focus of biomedical research shifted in response to major global events or emerging scientific challenges?
